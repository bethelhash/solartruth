Important disclaimers and methodology notes

INFORMATIONAL USE ONLY. Results provided by SolarTruth are for general informational and educational purposes only. Nothing on this website constitutes financial, tax, legal, or investment advice. Always consult a licensed financial advisor, tax professional, and qualified solar installer before making any solar investment decision.

ESTIMATES, NOT GUARANTEES. Solar production estimates, savings projections, payback periods, NPV, IRR, and all other financial outputs are estimates based on mathematical models and publicly available data. Actual results will vary based on installation quality, local weather patterns, shading conditions, utility rate changes, equipment performance, and many other factors beyond our control. No guarantee of any specific financial outcome is made or implied.

NEM AND UTILITY POLICIES. Net energy metering policies, utility rates, and solar incentive programmes change frequently. Data used in calculations reflects publicly available information as of the date of access. Verify current NEM policy, export rates, and utility tariffs directly with your utility company before relying on any figures shown here.

FEDERAL AND STATE INCENTIVES. Tax credit information is based on publicly available IRS guidance and state programme data. Tax credit eligibility depends on your individual tax situation. The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) requires sufficient federal tax liability to utilise. Consult a qualified tax professional before relying on any tax credit estimate. State incentive programmes are subject to funding availability, regulatory change, and eligibility criteria that vary by circumstance.

CLIMATE AND IRRADIANCE DATA. Solar production estimates use regional climate averages and validated physics models. Actual irradiance at your specific location may differ from regional averages used in calculations. Site-specific shading, roof condition, and microclimate factors are not fully accounted for in any remote estimate tool.

ROOF AND STRUCTURAL DISCLAIMER. This tool does not assess roof structural condition, load-bearing capacity, waterproofing integrity, or suitability for solar installation. A qualified roofing professional and structural engineer should assess your roof before any solar installation. Panel removal and reinstallation costs associated with roof repairs are not included in default calculations unless specifically enabled.

NO INSTALLER AFFILIATION. SolarTruth is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or financially connected to any solar installer, manufacturer, financier, or energy company. If you provide contact information, it may be shared with licensed solar installers in your area who have agreed to our partner terms. You may receive follow-up communications from those installers. You are under no obligation to purchase any product or service.

LEAD SHARING. By submitting your contact information, you consent to being contacted by up to three licensed solar installers in your service area. Your information will not be sold to data brokers or used for purposes unrelated to solar installation services. You may opt out at any time by contacting us.

EV CALCULATIONS. Electric vehicle consumption estimates are based on EPA fuel economy ratings and typical driving patterns. Actual EV energy consumption varies by driving style, climate, charging behaviour, and vehicle condition. EV-related solar sizing recommendations are illustrative only.

LIMITATION OF LIABILITY. To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, SolarTruth and its operators shall not be liable for any direct, indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages arising from use of this tool or reliance on any information provided herein. Use of this tool constitutes acceptance of these terms.

METHODOLOGY. Production estimates use a multi-stage physics-based calculation pipeline validated against US government solar benchmarks. Financial calculations use standard discounted cash flow methodology. Utility rates are sourced from EIA Form 861 (2023 annual survey). NEM policies are sourced from state Public Utility Commission orders and DSIRE (2024). Federal incentive data is sourced from IRS guidance and IRA 2022 statutory text.
